Roughly 28% of agentic PRs hit merge conflicts, and *semantic* conflicts
break green builds even when the textual merge succeeds. Two branches can
each pass tests alone and still break each other: one renames a function the
other starts calling. The merge train exists so that failure mode dies in a
worktree instead of on your trunk.

## Speculative validation

Completed branches queue as **cars**. Each car is validated not against
trunk as it is, but against the **predicted trunk**, meaning trunk plus
every car ahead of it in the queue:

<Fig caption="each car gates against trunk + all cars ahead of it" text={`
 trunk ──●
          \\
           ├─ car A   gates(trunk + A)       ✓
           ├─ car B   gates(trunk + A + B)   ✗ ← semantic conflict with A
           └─ car C   gates(trunk + A + C)   ✓ ← revalidated without B

           eject B alone ──▶ decision (or auto-heal)
           trunk ──▶ advances atomically to A + C — it was never red
`} />

Every combination runs your `gates` commands in a scratch worktree. Nothing
touches trunk until an entire train state is fully gated; then trunk
advances **atomically** to that state. A single failing car is **ejected
alone**; the cars behind it are revalidated without it rather than being
thrown away.

## The trunk-red probe

Before any fleet work merges, the reconcile loop probes trunk itself against
the gates in a scratch worktree. If trunk is already red, no car could ever
take the blame honestly, so all merges hold, and one decision lands:

> Trunk itself fails the merge gates (before any fleet work). Fix trunk, then
> exec again — completed work is held, not blamed.

The moment trunk is green again, that decision
[auto-resolves](../journal/#decisions-auto-invalidate) and held work merges
on the next reconcile.

## Ejection is a decision, not a deletion

An ejected car keeps its branch and its work. The ejection becomes either an
[auto-heal](../auto-heal/) (mechanical failures fix themselves forward) or a
[decision](../decisions/) with three options:

| Choice | What happens |
| --- | --- |
| `fix-forward` | A repair arm re-enters the *same branch* with the gate failure text as feedback: "fix exactly these failures, keep the completed work intact." |
| `retry-rebased` | Start the contract over on a fresh worktree cut from current trunk. |
| `park` | Settle the contract as not-landing; the run can complete `partial`. |

Eject reasons are distilled to the error lines that matter, never the temp
path the gate ran in.

## One train per repo

Concurrent runs (and campaign slices) queue at the trunk SubmitQueue-style: a
lock in `.git/kraken-train.lock` ensures one train advances a repo at a
time, with stale locks (30 min) broken automatically.

## Before the train: the conflict radar

The train is the last line. The **pre-write conflict radar** is the first:

```sh
kraken radar
```

A read-only `git merge-tree` simulation across all live `kraken/*` branches
surfaces files two arms are both touching while it's still one
conversation, instead of a merge failure an hour later. It's also exposed to
connected agent sessions as the `conflict_radar` tool of the
[Model Context Protocol server](../../guides/claude-code-plugin/).
