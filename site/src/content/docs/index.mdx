<div class="k-section">
  <p class="k-eyebrow">the missing pieces</p>
  <h2>What every fleet is missing</h2>
  <p class="k-lead">Orchestrating parallel agents is easy. Landing their work on a trunk that stays green — unattended — is the hard part. Kraken is built around exactly that.</p>
  <div class="k-cards">
    <article class="k-card">
      <span class="k-card-glyph">[■■■□]──▶ trunk</span>
      <h3>The gated merge train</h3>
      <p>Every change is validated against the <em>predicted</em> trunk — all cars ahead of it — behind your own build and test commands. A failing car is ejected alone; trunk only advances atomically to a fully-gated state. Semantic conflicts die on the train, not on main.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="k-card">
      <span class="k-card-glyph">judge: PASS (0.91)</span>
      <h3>A judge with hands</h3>
      <p>Checkpoints are scored end-state against the contract, with execution gates as evidence that outranks any claim. The judge inherits the worker's tool grants, so it can run <code>gh</code> and verify external side effects itself instead of taking the agent's word.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="k-card">
      <span class="k-card-glyph">seq 0412 · append-only</span>
      <h3>An event-sourced journal</h3>
      <p>Every contract, checkpoint, verdict, decision, and merge is an append-only event in SQLite. Crash and resume for free, replay any run, and keep an audit trail from goal to merge — who decided what, and why — as a side effect.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="k-section">
  <p class="k-eyebrow">quickstart</p>
  <h2>From goal to green trunk</h2>
  <div class="k-quickstart">
    <ol class="k-steps">
      <li><strong>Configure once.</strong> A <code>kraken.toml</code> names your trunk and the gate commands every merge must pass.</li>
      <li><strong>State the goal.</strong> A frontier-tier planner decomposes it into task contracts with owned paths and done-criteria.</li>
      <li><strong>Approve the plan.</strong> Plans pause for you; your annotations travel into the contracts.</li>
      <li><strong>Watch it land.</strong> Arms fan out in isolated worktrees, the judge scores each checkpoint, and the train merges what survives.</li>
    </ol>
    <div>

```sh
# in your repo
cat > kraken.toml <<'EOF'
trunk = "main"
gates = ["pnpm test"]     # every merge must pass these
max_parallel = 4
EOF

kraken run "Implement the delete-awareness spec in docs/specs/..."
kraken decisions                     # review the proposed plan
kraken decide <run> plan-<run> approve
kraken exec <run>                    # arms fan out, the train merges
```
